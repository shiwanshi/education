In India, education in the rural segments is not only important to eradicate poverty and illiteracy, but also for a variety of other social, economic as well as cultural and political reasons.

Benefits of Rural Education

Importance of Rural Education

Education unleashes new opportunities to access information and technology thereby enabling people to make better decisions. It allows them to understand the laws, policies, rights, duties, and various other schemes concerning them.

Education indirectly contributes to the Agriculture Sector.If people of rural areas will get skill-based education then they'll be able to build capacity and understand technology in a far better way.

Education and employment are almost synonymous with each other.If people in rural areas will get high-quality education, then they’ll be able to become more eligible for both public and private sectors.

Stats And Data

Rural India is far behind Urban India in every Indicator of Progress.A look at the literacy levels in India over last 3 decades from the same rural urban lens gives us more or less similar numbers. Rural literacy rate is much lower than the urban literacy rate. The point to be noted is the gender disparity in this area, where the urban female literacy rate is almost higher by 20% than the rural female.

Conclusion

Today, after almost 7 decades of independence, the existing policy dynamics has taken a toll on rural India. The villages take a backseat in almost every aspect of socio-economic analysis. We have created an economy of rich cities and poor villages, surging urban areas and decaying rural areas.
